About Linux

Linux is an open source OS used in various systems such as ATM machines, smartphones, servers, and supercomputers. It is known for its extreme stability, security, and flexibility.

Linux offers a wide range of distributions (distros) such as Ubuntu, Fedora, and CentOS, each catering to different user needs and preferences.

It is widely used in server environments due to its robustness and cost-effectiveness. Linux also provides a powerful command-line interface, making it a favorite among developers and system administrators. A huge benefit of Linux is that unless a kernel change is detected, it doesn't require a full system reboot. This allows for greater uptime and reliability in business critical systems.

Additionally, Linux has strong community support and a vast repository of open-source software, making it a versatile choice for various computing needs.
